About Me

Quote:

“The only thing a person can ever really do is keep moving forward. Take that big leap forward without hesitation, without once looking back. Simply forget the past and forge toward the future.” - Alyson Noel


I think this quote is both wrong and correct in different ways. Like most people, I haven’t always been successful in everything I have set out to do. Of course instead of letting failure deter us from a possible action, we should move forward. But the part of the quote I don’t agree with is forgetting the past and leaping forward without hesitation. Failure is a necessary part of the journey as it teaches us that we aren’t perfect and we still have room for improvement. Failure helps us look back on specific events and allows us to see what we could have done differently. By reflecting, we could see where we acted in the wrong way, so we can fix it and act in a better way next time. By simply forgetting the past, we would see no growth and risk failure again.

Employment

  • STEM SUCCESS

    • During my time at the Summer Youth Employment Program, I decided to challenge myself by choosing to work for Stem Success INC. rather than getting an office job. I stepped out of my comfort zone by teaching the STEM related skill of video production to the students of underprivileged communities. The STEM based skill that my boss had chosen for my group was video production and I was assigned to be one of two people to learn the skill and teach it to the students of that underprivileged community. I researched multiple sites and programs that dealt with the editing part of video production and made sure that the programs were free and very user-friendly by seeing the different reviews and comparing the layout of each program. My idea was that I should teach two different softwares as the students can get the basic features and the more advanced features. As I was teaching little kids about video production, I tried to make my lessons very easy to follow by not using big words and showing where the specific icons were. Instead of using just the PowerPoint, I would use the software live and they could see everything I was doing and could ask questions while I was doing it. Overall, the lessons I prepared were received well and I could tell that the children learned something as they sent videos of their own creation and I saw everything I taught them utilized, such as adding titles and adjusting audio.
